Sustainable Development Goal 6

Clean Water & Sanitation

Ensuring availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Safe water is a human right and foundational for health, education, gender equality, ecosystems, and resilient economies.

What is SDG 6?

SDG 6 aims to guarantee universal and equitable access to safe and affordable drinking water, adequate and equitable sanitation and hygiene, and to improve water quality and use efficiency while protecting ecosystems.

Key Targets

  • 6.1 Safe and affordable drinking water for all.
  • 6.2 Access to sanitation and hygiene; end open defecation.
  • 6.3 Improve water quality and wastewater treatment.
  • 6.4 Increase water-use efficiency and sustainable withdrawals.
  • 6.5 Integrated water resources management (IWRM).
  • 6.6 Protect and restore water-related ecosystems.

Why it matters

Water scarcity, pollution, and inadequate sanitation drive disease, reduce school attendance, and limit economic opportunity. Investing in water and sanitation yields high returns in health and productivity.

Solutions

Proven Approaches

  • Protect sources: Watershed restoration, buffer zones, pollution control.
  • Treat water: Point-of-use filters, chlorination, UV, and robust utility treatment.
  • Safely manage sanitation: Toilets, fecal sludge management, sewered and non-sewered systems.
  • Reuse & efficiency: Leak reduction, smart metering, water-efficient irrigation and industry.
  • Governance: Tariff reforms, pro-poor subsidies, IWRM with community participation.

Quick Wins

  • Handwashing stations in schools and clinics.
  • Fix leaks; install low-flow fixtures.
  • Separate greywater where feasible.
  • Protect springs and wells.

Innovation

Decentralized treatment, membrane bioreactors, nature-based solutions (constructed wetlands), and IoT monitoring.

How You Can Help

  1. Conserve at home: Fix leaks, take shorter showers, choose efficient appliances.
  2. Keep water clean: Dispose of oil, meds, and chemicals properly.
  3. Support WASH orgs: Donate or volunteer with water & sanitation groups.
  4. Advocate: Ask local leaders to invest in resilient water infrastructure.
  5. Learn & share: Teach others about SDG 6 and hygiene.
